birds fear death Interview

birds fear death is the Lofi Noise Pop project from by Dallas musician Kelly Wilhite. Kelly currently has 3 projects on her bandcamp, the latest of which just released under the name "livestream death compilation".

"Your music is incredibly personal, and confronts listeners with some very dark topics. How does it feel to expose your emotions to the world through music?"

"One of the most important parts about making music for me is talking about the things I'm feeling to the furthest extent possible. A lot of the time it feels like there really isn't any good place where you can just say the more dark or questionable things in your head, and so I like to turn to music for that. It's definitely nerve-wracking to have those things exposed to the entire world, but I guess throwing it into the indifferent void of the internet makes it a bit less so. I guess in summation, I just want to express the darker or more unspoken parts of my life, and music feels to me like the only place that feels right to do so.".

"Could you explain the name “bird's fear death”?

"Every animal has an innate desire to not die. Arguably the desire to not die coupled with the inevitability of death, is the reason why anyone does anything at all. The way birds express it specifically is very human-like which makes it more relatable to us, unlike other animals where we tend to personify them and their emotions to fit our own idea of death and fear."

"When did you first begin to make music, and how has your recording / writing process changed from when you first started compared to now?"

"I started making music when I was about 13 and then started bfd when i was 14, so really most of my evolution as a musician is cataloged in the stuff I’ve put out haha. In terms of recording, not much has changed. I’ve kinda just always opened up fl studio, hit record, and kept doing the whole song. Writing also isn't too different from how it was 4 years ago, but I think now I have a bit more of a grasp on what works and what doesn't when I try to put something together."

"How did “livestream death compilation” come to be? Were there any experiences / influences that made you decide to create it"?

"A lot of it was just wanting to try things out and make something a bit less derivative. Musically I just wanted to push the noise as far as I could go while also making something catchy and emotional. I also wanted to blur the lines a bit more between the electronic and more rock based parts of my music, and just tried to let go of my perceptions of what a song in whatever genre should sound like. Thematically, I wanted to explore a bit of the more aggressive feelings that come with self-destructive tendencies rather than being too focused on the melancholic aspects. As the title suggests, there's a large influence from how the internet has related to my youth and my emotions. Not necessarily in a way that implies that the internet is the cause of any specific problems but more so how they intertwine and play off each other."

"What music were you exposed to growing up? Do you think any of it has had an impact on your abrasive / noisy sound?

"To be completely honest I didn't really start listening to music until like 6th grade lol. Before that point I would just like occasionally listen to the undertale soundtrack or something. But definitely from the beginning of my legitimate interest in music I was really into the noisier or rougher stuff. Some of the first music I actually go into was shit like Fidlar or Death Grips, so all of that has definitely shaped my concept of how music works."

"Your music sounds more unique from record to record, do you think you’ve achieved your desired sound yet"?

"What I want to do is always changing, but there's always some consistent vision and I think this ep comes closer to that than my other stuff. I don’t just want to do this style forever, but at least for right now I think I'm happy with where I am."

"I’m really looking forward to what you create in the future, do you have anything planned so far"?

"I have a lot of miscellaneous ideas I'm working on, but I don't really have the big "next step" figured out yet. I do think that I want to do something more cohesive and compositionally interesting".
