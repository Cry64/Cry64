Snakehouse Interview

HAPPY PARADOX OF THE DARK LAND is a happy hardcore album by the artist Snakehouse. It came out on August 14th, 2022. It was released alongside an online art gallery of surreal cartoons. Snakehouse is known for envisioning a theoretical rave drug called “caveman”, and for releasing the remarkable song “I’m Ready to Be Loved” on the label Gabber Industries Berlin.

"When did the Snakehouse project begin?"

"In high school I bought a jacket that said “Snakehouse” at a store on North Loop. The idea was secretly planted by the universe right then and there. Fast forward to 2019. I was living in Tacoma. I had been producing obscure and mysterious synth music for about 2 years. I was also drumming in a psychedelic band called Lovely Bodies. Our singer took me to an all ages rave in Seattle one night. The sound was very dark and scary. While I was taking stock of my surroundings I had a thought, or maybe it was a sensation. It wasn’t until the next week that I realized what had happened. When I started recording a freeform hardcore trance beat, I became completely hypnotized. My brain was reacting to the rave sound. That was the moment I became Snakehouse.".

"How is this album different from the last ones?"

"DOWN WITH ANIME, UP WITH PIANO SONGS was all sampled based, whereas HAPPY PARADOX is mostly sample free, I sing on every song. LISTEN TO THE CAVEMAN was also sample free, but it was way more jungley and less happy."

"Did your creative process change between the last albums and this one?"

"I honed in on my favorite sounds, pianos, strings, hoovers, vocals, breaks, kicks. I spent a lot more time mixing, and I developed a new technique for mastering using Airwindows plugins. My day to day process was kind of the same except I spent a little more time birthing each core idea. I was feeling more confident."

"If I close my eyes and listen to this album, what are you trying to make me see?"

"A night sky viewed from a 500,000 BCE cave. The clouds and the moon are making you feel connected to the entire universe. Also you are not a human but rather an ancient human ancestor. That is why you can see things this way."

"What's the five-year plan for Snakehouse?"

"To develop a brand new form of euphoric rave music and contribute that style to others’ work. That should take about 3 years and with the remaining 2 years i’ll just throw parties for everyone who helped. But in all seriousness. For the next few years I just want to practice making the most euphoric rave music possible and working with all the producers who feel the same way."

"How does this new album, and the work of Snakehouse in general, relate to the history of rave music?"

"This album is based on the bouncy happy hardcore of the late 90s in the UK. And a little bit of the mid 90s sound with breakbeats and darker feeling. I like all the songs with rough sounding breakbeats, a bouncy kick, strings, and vocal melody. And I only really like the happy hardcore songs that are sort of sad. For example “Field of Dreams” from 1997 by Force and Styles which is what I based “Spirit Storm in The Field” on. My interpretation of that sound is sadder and weirder tho. I also love the Happy Hardcore mix of “Your Smile” by Charly Lownoise and Mental Theo. The melodies and chords are so melancholy. I tried to make melodies like that for this album. I also love “Perfect Dreams” by DJ Force and The Evolution which is from 1994. It definitely sounds like happy hardcore but there’s hardly anything happy in it at all. Overall, my music draws from old rave music that is super intense, euphoric, and sad. The only new rave music i’m interested in is underground stuff from my group of friends on discord and soundcloud. And a few of my friends in Austin like Dream Days and dreamgoth. Yes in fact I can get behind anything that’s dream-related."

"What do you like in the current music scene, and what do you want to see more of in the future?"

"Well I’m into the current online underground scene like I said before. People are truly unafraid to post the most bonkers shit. I just want to see more of that uninhibited creativity in the Austin scene."

"What's your sign?"

I’m a Taurus"

"What's your favorite hibachi spot in Austin?"

"Asahi Imports"

"Tell us about your upbringing."

"My dad is a Luthier and craftsman, my mom is an evolutionary anthropologist. Both of them are absurdly smart in different ways. My dad encouraged me to be a musician in his own laid back way. He kept instruments from all over the world in the house when I was growing up. Mainly African stringed instruments and some didgeridoos, which he built. His profession was building guitars, so I picked up on that and learned guitar when I was 11. I was taught by my brother Wade who played guitar in the band Iron Age. My mom taught me the value of academic exploration. But she didn’t pressure me into it. It slowly made its way into my lifestyle, and now I love learning about mind bending philosophy and science and math."

"What is the intention of the Snakehouse aesthetic?"

"To contrast relentless hardcore sounds with melancholy euphoric sounds. Visually I intend to create a surreal cartoon world about the distant past and the distant future."

"What is the relationship between the physical and online presences of Snakehouse?"

"Physically I am just a human being but online I am also a snake and a caveman. That is to say I am somewhat more mysterious online."

"Describe your collaborators and their contributions to this album."

"Well I was quite private with the musical part, I did that stuff on my own. But I got a ton of help with visual art from Gork, Dj Saratonin, Rainsdeaf, vivi, and wholesome_mooncloud. Gork made the album cover, Dj Saratonin made the background for the website and an overlay for the documentary, vivi helped with the website and drew one of the pictures, wholesome_mooncloud edited the documentary. And many others gave me feedback and listened to my mixes and such. I want to get Rainsdeaf and DJ Saratonin to contribute music to my next project though!!!"

"Why did you design the Happy Paradox website to go with this album?"

"I thought it would make the music more meaningful, also I haven’t really seen anyone do an online gallery like that so it's kinda unique. And it's an excuse to draw lots of weird things and develop my world. Thank you to everyone who helped me with it. I love you very much."

"Dream collaborations?"

"Sean Nicholas Savage, Connan Mockasin, DJ Lucas, Burial"

What meal should I eat while I listen to this album?""

"Trail Mix and Water. It is a very healthy option."

"What visual movie / television show should I be watching on mute while listening to this album?"

"August In The Water (1995)"
