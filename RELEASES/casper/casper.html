Casper McFadden Interview

Ever since their 2020 release "Audio Diary", Casper Mcfadden has pushed the boundaries of breakcore through a number of groundbreaking releases. The unrivalled energy radiating from Casper's production has positioned their music as iconic among the breakcore community, while continuously influencing the way producers approach the genre.

"Its hard to pinpoint where you stand in the world of electronic music, considering your sound spans across every corner of the dance spectrum. Where do you think you lean towards the most in terms of genre, and do you feel that genre even matters anymore?"

"I dont really think genre matters so much because everyone is kind of making a mix of everything right now".

"How do you go about blending different styles of music into a cohesive listening experience? Do you plan these things out, or would you say its all experimentation?"

"I am pretty bad at planning things so I kind of just throw different patterns up until I get one that I like!"

"Part 2 of the ABYSS CHASERS series released recently. How did this series come about in terms of your collaboration with MANAPOOL?"

I flew out to St. Louis last year and we basically made all the tracks together within a few days. Making music with her is really easy since were friends and we like all the same stuff lmao".

"The breakcore scene seems to revolve heavily around collectives. Could you tell me about the collectives you are in, and how you ended up joining them?"

"Right now its just 909 Worldwide and Chao Gardem! And I joined through friends I dont think Ill join anymore since discord kind of give me anxiety."

"Anime and video game culture seem to be a prominent theme in your music. Is this the result of how you grew up? If so, what other things growing up do you think have shaped your sound?"

"Yeah me and my sister were reading manga and watching anime as kids so its definitely influenced me heavily. Living in Chicago has inspired me the most Id say though."

"Can you recall your earliest memory of being exposed to breakcore / dnb?"

"It was probably like power puff girls or something like that lmao."

"Your live setup is unique to say the least What does your process look like when performing live with your setup?"

"its just me playing songs on my sp404 and using samples on my sp555 I use the fx on both to mix between the songs. Im going to be adding my ddj to that setup though."

"When performing 200bpm tunes live, crazy shit is bound to happen Can you recall the craziest thing youve witnessed while performing?"

"When I played in LA someone was running their face on the speaker while I was playing."

What is the rave scene like in Chicago, would you consider it popular?

"The pandemic hit us pretty hard, a lot of places closed down but the bars are starting to let us play so thats cool."

"What are you hoping to achieve in the future through your music, do you have anything planned so far?"

"I dont plan on anything I just love making music. I guess Id like to go on tour one day but Id honestly rather stay home with my music."
