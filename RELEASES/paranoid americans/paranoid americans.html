Paranoid Americans Interview

Paranoid Americans is the alternative indie rock project from Dallas musician Abe Randle. Abe currently has 9 projects on their bandcamp, the latest of which released under the name "The Cruel Dance".

"Your production & instrumentation is incredibly diverse and shifts from song to song. Do you think its important to have a diverse sound?"

"In my opinion, definitely yes. My belief is that the more different elements you experiment and are familiarized with, the more you can express yourself. When I first started creating music I was listening to totally different stuff and had totally different influences. Along with that overtime I experimented and found things I liked more musically and expanded upon them to get to my current place musically".

"How was your sound created, and how has it evolved into its current form?"

"When I started I was heavily inspired by DIY/garage rock music. One of the first things I loved musically was the skate three soundtrack, in about 7th grade. I was also inspired a lot by Car Seat Headrest. From there I experimented with that style with various other releases under other pseudonyms until I got to paranoid Americans with its first EP. After that I got heavily into bossa nova and other really rhythmic styles. I experimented with them on northern summer and I refined them on sun pop turning it into kind of a dance album with punk mixed in. Over the last six months Ive gotten much more into electronic music, older Childish Gambino, and old-school DnB. My last release, the cruel dance, was heavily inspired by this leading me to a current point of combining Punk/garage rock, rhythm/dance, and electronic/break beat. I am preparing a release for all of these coming up soon."

"Seeing as your music is very DIY, how do you approach songwriting and production? do you have a certain writing process set in line?"

"Typically, I approach a song first with drums then bass or vice versa. If I find a drum line I really really like most likely itll be a pretty good song. Same for bass. Sometimes what I like to do is sit on my bed and play different chord progressions on my guitar until I find something I really like then write it down. If Im slow to come up with a chord progression I typically take doo-wop progressions and change them slightly. Another thing I do when I cant come up with anything is borrow one of my friends instruments. For example, one of my songs entitled Country Song was recorded with a banjo I borrowed from my friend Owen. Theres one big thing Id like to mention though. Its that whenever I write its super hard for me to not write in the context of an album or other bigger work. If the songs Im writing in a session dont really line up in my opinion, I often scrap them."

"What is your local music scene like? You mentioned being in the same scene as birds fear death who is also very alternative. From an outsiders perspective it seems like a very progressive and fresh scene, is this true?"

"In my brain the DFW rock scene consists of two groups. There is one in Northern Dallas and one in central Dallas. The one in North DFW is a lot more hardcore punk based (notable bands Birds Fear Death, Rex Romanum, The Arches, The Ends) whereas the one in Central Dallas I feel has a more general garage/indie feel (notable bands are The Sayers, Paper Bears, Velvet Futon Club, Fishing in Japan, Ego Jones). I feel like the one in central Dallas is much more under developed though, mainly due to a lack of venues willing to host these bands. Both Im super thankful for, and I respect a lot and want to be more involved in in the future."

"Where do you see your local music scene heading towards in the future?"

"No freaking clue, though honestly I feel its been picking up a lot more momentum as of late. I wish I could say more on that."

"How do you like collaborating with other artists, is it something you wish to do more of? If so, who would you like to collaborate with?"

"I love collaborating with artists, I wish I could work with a lot more rappers and Id like to work with more locals in the future specifically. But I literally will collab with anyone as long as they know what they want from me."

"What are you hoping to achieve this year through music, do you have any releases planned so far?"

"I have a very big release, but its still in the works. Id like to get featured on a blog of some sort this year, thatd be really cool, or get a lot more opportunities to collab and produce for people."
